Guides

What you need to know before you sign

🏦

Financing

Dealership financing is convenient but rarely the cheapest option. Get pre-approved by your own bank or credit union first, so you have a real number to compare the dealer's offer against. Look past the monthly payment to the total loan cost — APR, term length, and any early-payoff penalty all matter.

🛡️

Insurance

Get an insurance estimate before you buy, not after. Sticker price, repair cost, top speed, and standard safety features all move your premium — sometimes more than the car's price tag does. Two cars at the same MSRP can cost very different amounts to insure.

📄

Warranty vs. Extended Service Contract

A factory warranty is included; an "extended warranty" (really an extended service contract) is a separate, optional, paid product. Before buying one, get clear on what's covered, for how long, whether it transfers if you sell the car, and your per-visit deductible.

🔄

Trade-In

Know your current car's trade-in value before you negotiate the new purchase — dealers may quietly fold a low trade-in offer into an otherwise good deal on the new car. Get an independent valuation first and negotiate the two numbers separately.

🔌

EV Charging & Incentives

Before buying an EV, map out home and workplace charging realistically, and check current federal and state incentives for your ZIP code — they can meaningfully change the effective price versus a comparable gas car.

⚠️

Dealer Add-On Watch List

Gap insurance, VIN etching, rustproofing, and paint protection are commonly pitched late in the process and can add thousands to a deal. None are automatically bad — but price gap insurance through your own insurer too, and ask for every add-on itemized and priced separately before you sign.
